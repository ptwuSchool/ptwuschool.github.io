Original Poem

A War To End All Wars


To die for your country
Is considered to be brave
But fighting in attrition
Is like being a slave.
A puppet of sorts
Being pulled by the strings
By those above
Arguing over petty things.
Is the glory of war really as they say?
To see the cruelty, there is only one way.

A few miles north
The houses ever burning
The fighting never ending
The killing -- quite concerning.
Atrocities made for an unknown reason,
Are the lives of innocent people
Truly worth this treason?
